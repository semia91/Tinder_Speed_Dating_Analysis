# Tinder Speed Dating Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes data from a massive speed dating experiment. The goal is to understand what actually drives attraction between two people and compare these results with what people *claim* they are looking for.

## 🎯 Business Questions Addressed
I used this dataset to answer 5 key questions:
1. **Least desirable traits:** What attributes do people care about the least?
2. **The Beauty Gap:** Is attractiveness more important in reality than on paper?
3. **Race vs. Interests:** Do shared hobbies matter more than shared racial backgrounds?
4. **Self-Perception:** Can people accurately judge their own "value" on the dating market?
5. **Timing:** Does the order of the date (first or last) impact success?

## 📊 Key Findings
* **Attractiveness is King:** While women claim to value intelligence, the correlation shows that physical beauty is the #1 factor for a second date.
* **Shared Interests win:** Having common hobbies is a stronger predictor of a "match" than belonging to the same ethnic group.
* **Bad Self-Judgment:** People are generally not very good at predicting how attractive they appear to others.
